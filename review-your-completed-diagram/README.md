**Learning objective:** By the end of this lesson, students will be able to refine a network diagram to ensure it is accurate, clear, and effectively communicates the structure and functionality of a network.

## Exercise

Congratulations on completing your first draft! Now, let's take a step back and review your work with a critical eye. The goal is to ensure your diagram is as clear and accurate as possible.

1. Take a look at your diagram as a whole. Does the overall layout make sense? Is it easy to follow the flow of data through the network?

2. Review each component. Does the shape accurately represent the device's function? Is it labeled clearly?

3. Check each connection. Are they labeled with the correct type (if known)?

4. Look for any missing components or connections. Compare your diagram to the network description and your list from when you identified the key network components.

5. Consider your use of color, style, and other visual elements. Do they enhance the clarity of your diagram, or do they clutter it?

6. Imagine you're seeing this diagram for the first time. Would you be able to understand the structure and function of General Co.'s network? If not, what could you change to improve clarity?

7. If you're unsure about any part of your diagram, refer back to the network description from your notes.

8. Make any necessary revisions to your diagram.

Once you're satisfied with your diagram, save it and export it as a PDF to submit.

Well done! You now have a complete, accurate diagram of General Co.'s network. This diagram will be an invaluable tool for documenting, troubleshooting, and planning changes to the network.
